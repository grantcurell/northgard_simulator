1. Time and economy scaling

* T1: One in-game month is 1 real-time minute. One year is 12 months, so 12 real-time minutes. The resource bar income is per tick, and each month has 6 ticks. That means 1 tick = 10 real-time seconds. ([Northgard Wiki][2])
* T2: Winter lasts 3 months, specifically December through February. A Northgard year has 12 months. A standard non-story game starts in year 800 AD. The editor documentation also maps month 0 to March, which is strong evidence that standard games start in March. ([Northgard Wiki][2])
* T3: The Town Hall’s “+3 kröwns” is per tick, not per month. The Town Hall page says it generates +3 kröwns and also states that this equals 18 per month and 216 per year; combined with the economy page’s “6 ticks per month,” that implies +3 per tick. Upgraded Town Hall does not list any change to kröwn production; the listed upgrade effects are advanced-building access, +20% population growth, and +2 population capacity. ([Northgard Wiki][3])

* V1: I did not find a public exact spawn formula, nor enough sourced measurement data points to fit it directly from documentation. What is documented is: villagers are generated by the Town Hall when happiness is nonnegative / positive, higher happiness makes them arrive faster, and larger total population slows arrival. Recruitment adds +25% permanent population growth speed, and upgraded Town Hall adds +20% population growth. So the exact coefficients remain unknown / not exposed and should stay calibratable. ([Northgard Wiki][6])
* V2: Spawn definitely stops when population reaches housing cap in practice, but I did not find a clean explicit current public sentence stating that rule directly. For Brundr and Kaelinn, I did not find an explicit housing/population rule on their unit pages, but they are categorized as Warband Units, and the generic Military Units page says special units such as warchiefs do not require warband nor housing and do not consume anything; however Brundr/Kaelinn are not called warchiefs on their own pages, so treat their housing rule as unknown unless you verify in-game. * W1: Confirmed: Villagers gather 4 food in summer and 2.4 in winter. Because a month has 6 ticks, that is 24 food/month in summer and 14.4 food/month in winter, or about 0.4 food/sec in summer and 0.24 food/sec in winter. ([Northgard Wiki][6])
* W2: Confirmed: Woodcutters produce +4 wood per tick. With 6 ticks per month, that is 24 wood/month, or about 0.4 wood/sec. I did not find a separate winter woodcutter production penalty; winter changes wood consumption, not woodcutter output, in the public sources I checked. ([Northgard Wiki][14])
* W3: Confirmed: each Merchant produces +2 kröwns per tick. With 6 ticks per month, that is 12 kröwns/month, or about 0.2 kröwn/sec. ([Northgard Wiki][10])
* W4: Confirmed: Miner rate is 1.92 stone or iron per month, or 2.88/month with Mining Efficiency. That converts to about 0.032/sec base and 0.048/sec with Mining Efficiency. ([Northgard Wiki][14])
